Extreme Weather over Antarctica and Beyond
Growing Influence of Atmospheric Rivers

Atmospheric Rivers (ARs), narrow corridors that transport concentrated moisture from lower to higher latitudes, significantly impact both mid-latitude and polar regions.

ARs in Antarctica can trigger surface melting, contributing to ice shelf collapse and threatening ice sheet stability, while extreme snowfall can offset ice loss. These complex dynamics create significant uncertainty in future Antarctic ice mass balance and sea level rise projections. In the western U.S., ARs are vital for water resources and drought relief but have caused over 80% of flood damage in the past 40 years, with risks increasing in a warming climate. Recent studies also highlight ARs’ impact on water supply and extreme weather in continental interiors.

My research conducts process-based studies to identify the dominant mechanisms driving AR-related extremes and to improve the assessment of their significant surface impacts.
